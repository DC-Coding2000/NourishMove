Detox Workouts

Key Principles:

  • Gentle, Flowing Movements: Exercises that stimulate circulation without overexerting the body, such as yoga, stretching, or Pilates.
  • Breathwork: Deep, diaphragmatic breathing helps oxygenate the body and improve lymphatic flow.
  • Sweating: Activities that induce sweating aid in releasing toxins through the skin.
  • Hydration: Important for flushing out toxins, so drink water during and after the workout.

    • Workout Structure

  • Frequency: 3-5 times per week.
  • Reps & Sets: 2-3 sets of 8-12 reps per exercise. Short rest periods between sets between 10 and 15 seconds.

    • Exercise 1 Forward Bend

    Instructions
  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  • Hinge at the hips, slowly bending forward.
  • Keep your back straight as you reach toward the floor.
  • Let your head and neck relax, and soften your knees if needed.
  • Hold the stretch, then slowly rise back to standing.

    • Exercise 2 Sun Salutations

    Instructions
  • Stand tall, inhale, and raise arms overhead.
  • Exhale, fold forward into a forward bend.
  • Inhale, step back into a plank, then lower into a push-up.
  • Inhale, lift chest into upward dog; exhale, push hips into downward dog.
  • Step forward, inhale to stand, and reach arms overhead again.

    • Exercise 3 Legs Up the wall

    Instructions
  • Sit next to a wall with one hip touching it.
  • Lie back and swing your legs up against the wall.
  • Adjust so your hips are close to the wall and legs are straight.
  • Relax your arms by your sides and soften your gaze.
  • Hold the position, breathing deeply for several minutes.

    • Exercise 4 Spinal Roll

    Instructions
  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart, arms relaxed.
  • Slowly tuck your chin to your chest.
  • Roll down one vertebra at a time, reaching toward the floor.
  • Let your head and arms hang at the bottom.
  • Roll back up slowly, stacking your spine until you're standing tall again.

    • Exercise 5 Cat-Cow Stretch

    Instructions
  • Start on all fours, hands under shoulders and knees under hips.
  • Inhale, arch your back, lifting your head and tailbone (Cow pose).
  • Exhale, round your back, tucking your chin and pelvis (Cat pose).
  • Flow smoothly between the two poses with each breath.
  • Repeat for several breaths, keeping movements slow and controlled.
