Energy Boost Workouts

Key Principles:

  • Moderate Intensity: Exercises that get the heart rate up without exhausting the body.
  • Breathing Techniques: Incorporating deep, rhythmic breathing to improve oxygen flow to muscles.
  • Full-Body Movements: Engaging multiple muscle groups helps circulate blood and boost energy.
  • Endorphin Release: Moderate exercise triggers the release of endorphins, which naturally boost mood and energy.

    • Workout Structure

  • Frequency: 3-5 times a week.
  • Reps & Sets: 2-3 sets of 10-15 reps per exercise.
  • Keep intensity moderate to avoid fatigue while boosting energy.

    • Exercise 1 Walking Lunges

    Instructions
  • Stand tall, feet hip-width apart.
  • Step one foot forward, lowering both knees to 90 degrees.
  • Push through front heel to stand, bringing back foot forward.
  • Step forward with the other leg into the next lunge.
  • Repeat, alternating legs as you move forward.

    • Exercise 2 Arm Circles

    Instructions
  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, arms extended out to the sides.
  • Make small circles with your arms going forward.
  • Gradually increase the size of the circles.
  • After several rotations, reverse direction, circling backward.
  • Keep core tight and arms straight throughout.

    • Exercise 3 Marching in Place

    Instructions
  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  • Lift one knee towards your chest while swinging the opposite arm.
  • Lower the foot and repeat with the other knee.
  • Keep alternating legs, lifting knees high.
  • Maintain a steady, rhythmic pace.

    • Exercise 4 Standing Side Bends

    Instructions
  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart, hands on your hips or raised overhead.
  • Slowly bend to one side, reaching towards your knee or ankle.
  • Engage your core as you return to standing upright.
  • Repeat the bend on the opposite side.
  • Alternate sides, keeping movements controlled.

    • Exercise 5 Plank to Downward Dog

    Instructions
  • Start in a plank position with hands under shoulders.
  • Push hips up and back into a downward dog position.
  • Hold for a moment, stretching your back and legs.
  • Lower hips back down into a plank position.
  • Repeat, moving smoothly between the two positions.
