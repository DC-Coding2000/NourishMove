The Origins of Yoga

Yoga is an ancient practice that originated in India over 5,000 years ago and has evolved into a complex system of physical, mental, and spiritual practices.

Origins

Yoga is believed to have originated on the banks of Lake Kantisarovar in the Himalayas, where Shiva, the first yogi, taught the Saptarishis, or "seven sages".

Early Records

The earliest written records of yoga are found in the Vedas, ancient Indian texts dating back to around 1500 BCE.

Patanjali

Patanjali, who wrote the Yoga Sutras around 200 BCE, is considered one of the most influential teachers in the history of yoga.

Hatha Yoga

Hatha yoga, which includes asanas (postures) and pranayama (breathing exercises), emerged between the ninth and 11th centuries.

Modern Yoga

In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, Swami Vivekananda introduced yoga to the West, and yoga masters began traveling to the West to attract followers.

Today

Yoga is practiced all over the world, and there are many different styles and approaches. Some people practice yoga for its physical and mental health benefits, while others practice it as a spiritual or religious practice.
