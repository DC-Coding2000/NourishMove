Muscle Gain Workouts

Key Principles:

  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increasing the weight or resistance used in workouts to challenge the muscles and encourage growth.
  • Compound Movements: Exercises like squats, deadlifts, bench presses, and pull-ups that engage multiple muscle groups at once.
  • High Protein Intake: Essential for muscle repair and growth, typically combined with a calorie surplus.
  • Rest & Recovery: Muscles grow during rest, so recovery between sessions is crucial.

    • Workout Structure

  • Frequency: 3-5 times per week, focusing on different muscle groups each day (e.g., legs, chest, back, shoulders).
  • Reps & Sets: 6-12 reps per set, with 3-5 sets per exercise.

    • Exercise 1 Push-Ups

    Instructions
  • With your legs extended back, place the hands below the shoulders, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Start bending your elbows and lower your chest until it's just above the floor.
  • Push back to the starting position. A 1-second push, 1-second pause, 2-second down count is ideal.
  • Repeat.

    • Exercise 2 Squats

    Instructions
  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Push hips back, as if sitting in a chair.
  • Lower down until thighs are parallel to the floor.
  • Keep chest up and knees behind toes.
  • Push through heels to stand back up.

    • Exercise 3 Lunges

    Instructions
  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  • Step one foot forward, lowering hips until both knees are bent at 90 degrees.
  • Keep front knee above ankle and back knee off the ground.
  • Push through front heel to return to standing.
  • Repeat on the other leg.

    • Exercise 4 Planks

    Instructions
  • Start on all fours, elbows under shoulders.
  • Extend legs back, balancing on toes.
  • Keep body in a straight line from head to heels.
  • Engage core, avoiding sagging hips or raised back.
  • Hold the position, breathing steadily.
  • Hold the position, Until 2 minutes completed do this two times.

    • Exercise 5 Pike Push-Ups

    Instructions
  • Start in a downward dog position with hips raised.
  • Hands shoulder-width apart, head between arms.
  • Lower head towards the floor by bending elbows.
  • Keep back straight and hips high.
  • Push through hands to return to the start.
