Weight Loss Workouts

Key Principles:

  • Calorie Deficit: Burning more calories than you consume is key to fat loss.
  • High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT): Short bursts of high-intensity exercises followed by rest periods. HIIT is highly effective for burning calories in a short amount of time.
  • Cardiovascular Endurance: Activities like running, cycling, or swimming to increase heart rate and calorie expenditure.
  • Strength Training: Builds lean muscle, which helps increase metabolism and burn more calories at rest.

    • Workout Structure

  • Frequency: 4-6 times per week.
  • Reps & Sets: 12-15 reps per set, with 3-4 sets per exercise. Short rest periods between sets.

    • Exercise 1 Burpees

    Instructions
  • Start standing, then squat down and place hands on the floor.
  • Jump feet back into a plank position.
  • Do a push-up, chest to the floor.
  • Jump feet back toward hands.
  • Explosively jump up, reaching arms overhead.

    • Exercise 2 Jumping Jacks

    Instructions
  • Start standing with feet together and arms by your sides.
  • Jump feet out wide while raising arms overhead.
  • Jump feet back together, lowering arms to your sides.
  • Keep knees slightly bent and core engaged.
  • Repeat continuously at a steady pace.

    • Exercise 3 Squat Jumps

    Instructions
  • Start in a squat position with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Lower down, keeping chest up and knees behind toes.
  • Explode upwards, jumping as high as possible.
  • Land softly back into a squat position.
  • Repeat immediately for the next jump.

    • Exercise 4 High Knees

    Instructions
  • Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  • Lift one knee to your chest while swinging opposite arm.
  • Quickly switch legs, lifting the other knee.
  • Continue alternating knees at a rapid pace.
  • Keep core tight and stay on the balls of your feet.

    • Exercise 5 Lateral hops

    Instructions
  • Stand with feet together, knees slightly bent.
  • Hop to one side, landing softly on both feet.
  • Immediately hop back to the other side.
  • Keep your core engaged and knees bent.
  • Repeat, maintaining a quick and steady rhythm.
